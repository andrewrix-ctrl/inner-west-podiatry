Five small habits for happier feet this month

You don’t need a complete life overhaul – just a few small changes your feet will quietly thank you for.

1. Add one extra short walk on most days

Choose a time that works for you – after breakfast, after work, or after dinner – and add 5–10 minutes of gentle walking.

If 10 minutes feels too much, start with 3–5 minutes and build up slowly.

☐ I’ve chosen a time of day that suits me.

2. Give your “at home” shoes a check-up

Many people have an old pair of slides or slippers they only wear at home – and those are often the least supportive shoes they own.

Take a moment to check: do they bend in the middle, twist easily, or feel worn out? If so, it might be time to upgrade.

☐ I’ve checked my at-home shoes and made a plan if they need replacing.

3. Stretch your calves while the kettle boils

Tight calves can add to heel and forefoot discomfort. A gentle calf stretch while you’re waiting for the kettle or microwave is a simple way to build a routine.

Keep it comfortable – you should feel a stretch, not pain. If you have an existing injury, ask a health professional before starting new exercises.

☐ I’ve tried a gentle calf stretch this week.

4. Rotate your shoes through the week

Wearing the same pair all day, every day means your feet only experience one pattern of support and pressure.

If you can, rotate between two or more comfortable, supportive pairs through the week to share the load.

☐ I’ve chosen at least two “good” pairs to rotate between.

5. Book help if pain is hanging around

If you’ve had foot or ankle pain for more than a few weeks, or it’s stopping you doing normal daily activities, it’s worth getting it checked.

Podiatrists are trained to assess and manage foot and lower limb problems – you don’t need to wait until things are “really bad”.

☐ I’ve made a note to book an appointment if my pain doesn’t settle.
