About Inner West Podiatry

Inner West Podiatry is a friendly, community-focused clinic in Glebe. We look after feet and lower legs for people of all ages – from first steps and school sport to long work days and long-term conditions like diabetes.

A podiatry clinic built for real life

Inner West life is busy. There are hills to walk, kids to chase, dogs to run, trams and trains to make. Sore feet or aching legs make all of that harder than it needs to be.

Our clinic is set up to be calm, welcoming and practical. We take the time to listen to what's been going on, examine your feet and lower limbs properly, and explain clearly what we think is happening and what we can do about it.

The goal isn't just to get you through this week's pain, but to keep you walking comfortably around Glebe, Annandale, Forest Lodge and the broader Inner West for the long term.

About Bethan Kinning

Bethan Kinning is an Australian-based podiatrist with over 17 years of experience. She graduated from the University of Wales in 2006 with a Bachelor of Science (Honours) in Podiatry and began her career in the UK's National Health Service.

In 2008 she returned to Australia and has worked in private practice ever since, building deep experience across all facets of podiatry – from general foot care and nail/skin problems to biomechanical issues and long-term conditions. She enjoys working with patients of all ages, from children through to older adults.

Bethan is a member of the Australian Podiatry Association and is a registered provider for the Department of Veterans' Affairs and the Medicare Enhanced Primary Care (EPC) Program.

She established and runs Sydney Inner West Podiatry in Glebe, where she's known for her patient-centred approach – creating individual, effective treatment plans and taking the time to share knowledge in a gentle, practical way so people feel confident looking after their own foot health.

What you can expect at your first visit

  • A relaxed, one-on-one appointment with your podiatrist.
  • A chance to talk through your symptoms, daily activities and health history.
  • Examination of your feet and lower limbs, and often a walking or standing assessment.
  • A clear explanation of what we've found, in plain language.
  • A treatment plan that fits your life – not a one-size-fits-all printout.

If you haven't seen a podiatrist before, our Fees & FAQs page explains how visits work, what to bring and how rebates or chronic disease plans can apply.
