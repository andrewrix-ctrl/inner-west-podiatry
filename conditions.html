Conditions & treatments

Below is an overview of common problems we see in the clinic and how podiatry can help. It's not an exhaustive list – if something is bothering your feet or lower legs, it's worth asking.

Heel & arch pain

Heel and arch pain is often worse with the first few steps in the morning or after sitting, and can make every step feel sharp or aching. The most common diagnosis is plantar fasciitis, but there are other causes too.

What's going on?

The plantar fascia is a strong band of tissue running along the sole of the foot. When it is overloaded or irritated, it can become sore where it attaches to the heel or along the arch. Tight calves, sudden changes in activity, footwear and foot posture can all play a role.

How we treat it

  • Careful assessment of your walking pattern, footwear and activity levels.
  • Stretching and strengthening plans tailored to you.
  • Advice on footwear and activity modification while things settle.
  • Orthoses (insoles) where needed to share load more comfortably.

Forefoot & toe problems

Pain in the ball of the foot, toes drifting or crossing, or a burning sensation between the toes are all common reasons people come to see us.

Common conditions

  • Morton's neuroma (nerve irritation between the toes).
  • Metatarsalgia (general forefoot pain).
  • Bunions (hallux valgus) and toe deformities.

How podiatry can help

  • Footwear advice to reduce pressure and irritation.
  • Padding or orthoses to offload sore areas.
  • Exercise programs to improve strength and control.
  • Discussion of when imaging or surgical opinions may be useful.

Skin & nail care

Sometimes small problems – a thick nail, stubborn corn or cracked heel – can make everyday walking painful. Regular podiatry care can make a big difference.

What we see often

  • Ingrown or involuted toenails.
  • Corns and callus on toes or weight-bearing areas.
  • Fungal nails (discoloured, thickened nails).
  • Cracked heels.

What we can do

  • Gentle reduction of thickened nails, callus and corns.
  • Treatment options for ingrown nails, including minor procedures if needed.
  • Advice on creams, antifungal treatments and home care.

Children's feet & growing legs

Many parents aren't sure what's "normal" when it comes to kids' feet and walking. We're happy to check little feet and give clear, reassuring explanations.

Common reasons to see us

  • Flat feet or very high arches.
  • Tripping, clumsiness or unusual walking patterns.
  • Heel pain in active kids (often Sever's disease).
  • Toe-walking or in-toeing.

How we work with kids

  • Age-appropriate assessments and explanations.
  • Footwear advice for school and sport.
  • Exercises and activity ideas that fit into their week.
  • Monitoring over time where needed.

Diabetes & long-term foot health

Diabetes, circulation changes and nerve changes can all increase the risk of foot problems. Regular podiatry checks help pick up issues early and keep you on your feet.

Why checks matter

We look at blood flow, nerve function, foot shape, skin and nails to understand your risk and spot early changes. Catching problems early means simpler treatments and fewer complications.

What we do in a diabetes foot check

  • Check pulses and circulation.
  • Test protective sensation (feeling) in the feet.
  • Look for pressure areas, callus and early skin changes.
  • Plan regular care and home strategies to reduce risk.
