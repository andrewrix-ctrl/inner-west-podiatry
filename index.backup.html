Latest foot health news

Evidence‑based insights, explained in plain English.

For patients & podiatrists

  • Are your "comfy" shoes secretly tiring out your feet?

    Theme: Everyday footwear Audience: Patients

    Many people choose ultra‑soft, squishy shoes because they feel comfortable in the shop. The catch? If your shoes do all the work, your foot muscles can switch off. Beth explains how to choose shoes that feel good and still let your feet stay strong – including a simple 3‑step check you can do in the store.

  • Heel pain in the morning: when to stretch, when to seek help

    Theme: Plantar heel pain Audience: Patients

    That first painful step out of bed isn’t just “getting older”. We walk through common causes of heel pain, simple routines you can try at home, and clear signs it’s time to book in with a podiatrist rather than continuing to “wait and see”.

  • Marathon season: load, recovery and realistic training feet can handle

    Theme: Sports podiatry Audience: Patients & podiatrists

    Training volume often jumps faster than feet can adapt. Beth outlines a foot‑friendly structure for ramping up distance, what to do when niggles appear, and how podiatrists can help runners balance performance goals with sensible tissue loading.

  • For clinicians: quick screen for footwear red flags in under 2 minutes

    Theme: Clinical practice Audience: Podiatrists

    A practical chair‑side checklist Beth uses to spot footwear issues during busy consults. We cover sole stiffness, heel counter strength and in‑shoe volume – plus simple language you can use so patients understand the “why” behind your recommendations.
