Patients · Foot health explainer

Why our feet love regular movement

Gentle, regular movement is one of the best things you can do for your feet – even if you’re not a runner.

Feet were made to move

Each foot has 26 bones, 33 joints and more than 100 muscles, tendons and ligaments. They’re designed to move, adapt and respond to the ground under you.

When we spend long stretches sitting, or only take short trips between the car, desk and couch, those tissues don’t get the regular “gentle loading” they like.

How movement helps foot health

A small amount of regular movement can help to:

  • Support circulation – your calf and foot muscles act like a pump, helping blood move back up the leg.
  • Maintain strength – muscles that are used regularly stay stronger for longer.
  • Support balance – regular movement keeps the small stabilising muscles and sensors in your feet “awake”.
  • Keep joints comfortable – gentle motion encourages normal joint nutrition and reduces stiffness.

“I’m not very active – is it too late to start?”

Not at all. The trick is to start gently and consistently, not perfectly. For many people, that means:

  • Adding an extra 5–10 minutes of walking to most days.
  • Breaking up long sitting periods with a short walk around the house or office.
  • Choosing the stairs once or twice a day, if your body allows.
Beth’s Inner West tip: if “exercise” feels intimidating, think “movement snacks” – small bursts of movement you sprinkle through your day.

What if my feet already hurt?

If you’re dealing with heel pain, forefoot pain or other foot issues, it’s still important not to swing from “rest” to “overdo it”.

Instead:

  • Keep movements short and gentle at first.
  • Pay attention to next‑day soreness – that’s your guide for what your feet can handle right now.
  • Book in with a podiatrist if pain is persistent, sharp, or stopping you from doing normal daily activities.

Regular movement doesn’t need to mean long runs or gym memberships. For most people, a slightly more active day‑to‑day routine is enough to make a real difference to how their feet feel.
