Children's feet & growing legs

Kids' feet go through a lot of change as they grow. It's common for parents to have questions about what's normal, what might need help and how to choose good shoes for busy little feet.

When to have your child's feet checked

  • They trip or fall more than other children their age.
  • They avoid walking long distances or ask to be carried.
  • You notice very flat feet or very high arches.
  • They complain of sore heels, arches, knees or shins after sport.
  • You're simply not sure whether their walking pattern is typical.

Common paediatric foot conditions

  • Sever's disease (heel pain in active children).
  • In-toeing or out-toeing.
  • Toe-walking.
  • Growing pains in legs and feet.
  • Flat feet that cause pain or fatigue.

What happens in an assessment?

A children's appointment is relaxed and age-appropriate. We'll usually:

  • Chat about your child's activities, sports and any pain or tiredness they've mentioned.
  • Look at their feet, legs and posture, both standing and moving.
  • Watch them walk (and sometimes run) to see how everything is working together.
  • Explain in simple language what we're seeing and what it means.

Many variations are completely normal and simply need monitoring rather than treatment. Where we do recommend exercises, footwear changes or orthoses, we'll explain the reasoning and what kind of difference you can expect.

Shoes, sport & school

Choosing shoes for growing feet can be confusing. During your visit we can look at the shoes your child is currently wearing and give practical advice about what to look for in school shoes, sport shoes and everyday footwear.

We can also suggest ways to support kids who love their sport but are being held back by pain – whether that's through load management, exercises or simple changes at training.
